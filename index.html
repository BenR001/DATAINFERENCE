Heart Disease Prediction Dataset

DESCRIPTION

My dataset was taken from the University of California Irvine data repository, it is used to predict heart disease.

DETAILS

Patients were classified as having or not having heart disease based on cardiac catheterization, the gold standard.
If they had more than 50% narrowing of a coronary artery they were labeled as having heart disease.
There are 270 patients and there are 13 independent predictive variables or column attributes.
The attributes are explained here and shown below.

1. #3 (age)
2. #4 (sex)
3. #9 (cp)
4. #10 (trestbps)
5. #12 (chol)
6. #16 (fbs)
7. #19 (restecg)
8. #32 (thalach)
9. #38 (exang)
10. #40 (oldpeak)
11. #41 (slope)
12. #44 (ca)
13. #51 (thal)
14. #58 (num) (the predicted attribute)

Prelimitary Analysis

Histograms:

The first histogram is describing the frequency of ages between each patient. We can observe that
most of the patients are between the ages of 50 and 60. We can also observe a normal distribution.

This barplot describes The frequency of Sexes between the patients. 0 indicates women and 1 for men.
We observe that there is much more men in the data than women.

This histogram describes the frequency of systolic bloodpressures between the patients. We can observe
the typical bloodpressure is between 110 and 140. We can also suprisingly see patients with bloodpressures that would be considered
to be in a hypertensive crisis which would call for an emergency room visit. We would consider this an outlier.

This graph describes the frequency of types of chest pain felt by the patients. 1 is typical angina, 2
is atypical angina, 3 is non-anginal pain, and 4 is asymptomatic. While most of the patients were asymptomatic, the 2nd most
frequent was non-anginal pain which is associated with pain in the chest that is not related to the heart.

Milestone 6

Values:

1. The mean and median values are different. The mean values for Age (mean_value) represents the average Age of the individuals.
This suggests that the individuals are, on average,relatively old. As for the blood pressure mean (mean_value2) this suggests that
the individuals have relatively high blood pressure. The median values are different, however this is not
suprising as it was not expected to be similar due to taking two different measures.

2. As for the standard deviation (SD), The SD of the ages is lower than the blood pressure column. This
means that the values are likely to be more spread out in the blood pressure column than the ages column.
In other words, the values in ages are more clustered together around the mean than in bloodpressure.
